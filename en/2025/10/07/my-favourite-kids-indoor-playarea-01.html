A Great Kids Indoor Playground Near Metro Vancouver: Wonderland Kids Indoor Play Center

MetroMama Oct 7, 2025
  • indoor
  • tsawwassen
  • kids cafe

🏰 Visit to Wonderland Kids Indoor Play Center

📍 Location: 328-4949 Canoe Pass Way, Tsawwassen, BC V4M 0B2
💰 Price: 1 Child + 1 Adult → $25.99 + GST
⏰ Playtime: Unlimited on weekdays, 2-hour limit on weekends

🎡 Layout Overview


(This is a simplified floor map I drew myself — not to scale.)

As of 2025, the play center is divided into three main zones:
1️⃣ Main Play Area (included with admission)
2️⃣ Add-on Zone 1: Rainbow Haven
3️⃣ Add-on Zone 2: Ninja Jump

The main play area alone is more than enough for most kids.
If it’s your first visit, there’s really no need to pay for the add-ons right away.
(Of course, the owner will recommend them — just smile and say, “Next time, thank you 😊.”)

🧩 Main Play Area (Maze)


(This photo was taken from the back of the main area. You can find more photos on Google Maps reviews.)

The centerpiece is a massive maze-like play structure filled with slides in every direction.
Each child finds their own favorite route through the tunnels and ramps.

Behind it, there’s a block-building zone and a small toddler-only area (for ages 3 and under).
The toddler zone isn’t very large, so it may not be worth visiting just for that age group —
but if you have siblings of different ages, it’s perfect for everyone to enjoy together.


(A quiet block-building area where kids can focus and play calmly.)

The facility has plenty of restrooms, and they’re kept very clean thanks to frequent staff checks.
There’s also a bouncy castle section, where kids can literally jump until they run out of energy 😆

🌈 Add-on Zone 1: Rainbow Haven

This zone features a web-like climbing structure made of soft netting —
something you might often see in Korean-style indoor playgrounds.
My kids weren’t too interested in it, but some children loved it so much
they stayed there almost the entire time.

🧗 Add-on Zone 2: Ninja Jump

This section includes trampolines, small climbing walls,
and various balance challenge courses — perfect for energetic kids.

Our children absolutely loved it and spent most of their time here,
burning off every bit of energy they had.

If your kids enjoy physical play, the add-on fee is totally worth it.
But since these zones aren’t included in the base ticket,
you can always explore first and decide later.

☕️ Café & Rest Area


(The kids in this photo have been removed with a photo tool, so you might notice some smudges.)

Food and drinks are only allowed in the designated dining area outside the play zone.
Non-playing adults can also sit there to relax and keep an eye on the kids.

Outside food is allowed, and there are plenty of options nearby —
A&W, Firehouse Subs, and Starbucks are all within walking distance.
One parent can even pop over to Tsawwassen Mills to grab snacks or meals.
No worries — there’s no shortage of food around here!

🧒🏻 Overall Impression

Although FlyO’Land Kids’ Playcenter inside Tsawwassen Mills is more well-known,
I personally preferred Wonderland Kids Indoor Play Center.

It’s especially great for kids who love running and climbing.
The maze is big and open enough that parents can join in easily —
which isn’t always the case in many other indoor playgrounds.

Because it’s spacious, the air circulation feels much better too.

I’d honestly call this one of the best indoor playgrounds in the Greater Vancouver area
